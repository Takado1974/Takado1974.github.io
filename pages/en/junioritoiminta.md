# Junior Activities

**Junior activities in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Submission Wrestling at Takado consist of sports, self-defense, and competition in good company!**

All groups have room for new practitioners. Welcome to get to know the discipline and the instructors!

Juniors train at Takado in three different age groups: Koalas (ages 6-9), Pandas (ages 10-12), and Youth (ages 13-15). Information about the content and goals of the training can be found further down the page. Support can also be applied for regarding the junior activity training fees. Junior training is guided by shared values and operating principles to which everyone commits. Before the actual junior groups, children can start getting to know the discipline in the Family Jiu-Jitsu group.

#### Important Values for Us:

* **Equality and Fairness** among everyone.
* **Safe Physical and Psychological Training Environment**: Training at Takado produces joy and positive experiences. I can do it, I am learning!
* **Responsibility**: Takado's coaches follow the values of junior activities and are responsible for the safety of the training.

#### Operating Principles Guiding the Training:

* **Training should be fun**, because we learn things that we enjoy.
* **Training must be safe.** Martial arts are less risky compared to many other sports. Training is safe and fun when everyone takes care of their own and their training partner's safety.
* **The gym is an equal place for everyone, where everyone is respected.** Takado does not tolerate bullying, inappropriate language, or exclusion from the group. Everyone is a jujutsuka, and everyone is welcome to train together as long as respect and appreciation for the discipline and training partners are maintained. Misuse and intentional harm lead to exclusion from training and the club.

#### Junior Training Groups and Their Goals

Juniors train in three groups.

##### Koalas

The youngest training group is aimed at ages 6-9. The goal of training in this group is to learn good motor skills and develop physical performance typical of the discipline.

In addition to basic physical skills, the teaching emphasizes the learning of social skills. Group work is practiced through various games and play, and the rules of the discipline are introduced through partner exercises. Learning martial arts is a form of partnership. In the group, everyone trains with everyone else, and together we create a fun and supportive atmosphere for learning.

##### Pandas

The training group is primarily aimed at ages 10-12 but also at slightly younger children who want to focus on active competition. In this group, muscle and coordination exercises are clearly specific to the discipline and are intended to support the performance of techniques. The focus of the sessions is on basic techniques. In addition to physical exercises, the goal is to develop mental readiness for combat sports. At Takado, everyone is allowed to practice Jiu-Jitsu based on their own starting points and pursuing personal goals.

BJJ and submission wrestling are competitive sports, and competition is encouraged at Takado, but each practitioner decides for themselves whether they want to compete or not. To be a good combat athlete, one must be able to respect the opponent and act as a good partner, because without good opponents, there is no opportunity to develop. The flip side of this is that one must also learn how to lose. The goal for this age group is to understand the combat athlete's responsibility for their own and their opponent's safety and for being a good partner.

##### Youth

The junior group is primarily aimed at 13-15-year-old youth. At this age, many changes occur in a young person's physique. These changes partly enable near-adult-level physical performance, but for many, a growth spurt also brings challenges as coordination must adapt to changed body proportions.

The relationship between learning and teaching also changes. Instructors teach advanced techniques in addition to basic ones, but the goal is for the youth to increasingly identify their own technical shortcomings and actively develop their skills in their desired direction. BJJ/Submission Wrestling is such a versatile discipline that it offers everyone their own way to fight. Everyone's body proportions are different, and on the threshold of adulthood, these differences can be very large. Because of this, the adoption of techniques that suit oneself is emphasized.

In addition to improving technical skill and physical performance, this age group is also taught matters related to self-defense. Most martial arts are also self-defense disciplines, but self-defense is much more than just technical skill. Mental qualities and self-confidence enable getting out of threatening situations without resorting to physical means. Furthermore, legal responsibilities and obligations must be understood as one approaches the age of criminal responsibility.

Learning is measured at the end of the autumn and spring seasons in belt tests. Children and juniors use five belt ranks: white, gray, yellow, orange, and green. "Stripes" are collected on each belt based on the progress made during the training season. The goal for youth who have trained for a longer time is that when they move to adult training, they would be ready to take the adult blue belt test.
