# About the Organization

Takado ry is a non-profit association that offers training in traditional martial arts for both children and adults. Takado's aim is to provide its members with a versatile, goal-oriented, and safe environment for practicing disciplines related to combat sports, self-defense, and fighting skills across the domains of knowledge, skill, and attitude. Through our activities, our members learn to know themselves better, are able to improve their physical and mental wellbeing, and to live more fully and safely in our society.

Takado's most important means of advancing these aims and goals is organizing regular training sessions led by expert instructors. To fulfill its purpose, the association also organizes competitions as well as informational and educational activities. The association has no paid staff; the board, instructors, and other people in positions of responsibility work on a voluntary basis. The association finances its activities mainly through membership and training fees, as well as through various grants that can be applied for. The association has the right to accept donations.

Takado's disciplines are European jujutsu, Brazilian jujutsu, escrima (Sikado), and kyudo. Takado is one of the first—if not the very first—Finnish clubs to begin serious training in Brazilian jujutsu. Training takes place almost daily throughout the year, and the club organizes camps and seminars related to discipline training for its members every year.
